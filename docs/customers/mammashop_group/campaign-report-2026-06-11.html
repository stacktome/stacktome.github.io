Why attribution matters — and why organic reviews dominate in June

  • Attributed = review matched to a campaign invite via order_id. The customer followed the invite link, or their order ID in the review platform matched the one we sent the invite to.
  • Organic = review submitted without an order_id match. This includes: customers who found the review platform independently, customers who left an order_id field blank, and customers driven by a strong emotion (positive or negative) who didn't use the invite.
  • June is only 11 days old. Invites sent in early June typically produce reviews 7–14 days later — most attributed reviews from the current send wave haven't come in yet. The 4,221 June sends are in-flight.
  • Organic reviews that arrive proactively skew negative — customers with unresolved issues are more motivated to post without an invite.

Early June signal: 90.5% organic rate vs 28.6% in May reflects the early-month timing more than a structural change. However, the 3 negative organic reviews that have arrived already show the same CS and fulfilment themes from May — these are not one-off incidents.

Emerging June Themes

CS process loops: Two mammashop complaints (return refund, broken product video) show CS agents not reading prior email context in the same thread. Both customers have been going back and forth for 2+ weeks. These likely indicate a ticketing / handoff issue rather than individual agent failures.

Wrong-customer package (Naturebaby): Second incident in two months. May saw a Hoppekids bed with another customer's name on packaging. June has a Naturebaby/Hoppekids bed with wrong tracking identity ("Mathilde"). May be the same fulfilment issue resurfacing — worth a warehouse check on returns reprocessing.

What's working: The 10 organic 5★ mammashop reviews suggest strong baseline product and delivery satisfaction for the majority. The 2 attributed reviews (both 5★) confirm campaign-invited customers remain highly satisfied when they do respond.

Expectation for end of June: Attribution rate will recover to ~60–70% as the June invite wave converts. Assuming same campaign-customer behaviour as May, avg rating should hold 4.3–4.5 group-wide if the open complaints are resolved.
