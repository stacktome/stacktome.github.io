Root Cause Analysis — Delivery Crisis

Delivery Failure — Four Compounding Factors
Factor What happened Review signal
1. Holiday spike Grundlovsdag (Jun 5) created a gift-buying surge with hard deadlines. Multiple customers ordered specifically for "Fars dag" and expected delivery by Jun 5. ★ Jun 4–5 worst days
2. False "dag-til-dag" promise Website advertised same/next-day delivery. Customers placed orders trusting this — some within 1.5h of the stated cutoff. Orders took 7–14 days. 8 of 12 one-star reviews cite this explicitly
3. "Ekspreshåndtering" fraud Website offered "free express handling" as an upsell incentive (buy 6 more bottles). Customers who unlocked this received no priority treatment. One review documents this in detail: order placed May 27, not received Jun 8 (12 days later). 2-star review Jun 8: "Hvad betyder ekspreshåndtering egentlig?"
4. Carrier breakdown (DAO / WebrinQ) At least two carriers implicated. One customer was told by DSF that order was "on the pallet" and would ship Wednesday — but DAO had not received the parcel by Thursday. WebrinQ told the same customer directly that they had not received the shipment. Direct carrier contradiction documented Jun 5 review

Recovery Signal — June 9

Full Recovery on June 9 (avg 5.00)

All 4 reviews on June 9 were 5-star and every single one praised fast delivery — a complete reversal:

  • "Lækker vin med gratis levering, så bliver det ikke bedre."
  • "Dejlig vin, hurtig levering."
  • "bestilt i går, leveres i dag"
  • "Nem bestilling, hurtig hjemmelevering."

This confirms the delivery backlog was a time-bounded crisis tied to the Grundlovsdag holiday surge, not a structural logistics failure. The underlying product and service quality remains strong.

What's Working — 5-Star Themes

The 51 five-star reviews share consistent themes when they include text:

  • Fast delivery — "hurtig levering", "bestilt i går, leveres i dag" (when logistics works)
  • Evening delivery — specifically valued by working customers
  • Good prices — "vine til en skarp pris"
  • Easy ordering — website experience is positive
  • Tracking — "kunne følge med i, hvornår ordren blev afleveret"

The product is not the issue. Every 1-star review that mentions wine quality notes the wine is fine. Delivery is the sole driver of dissatisfaction.

Recommendations

ActionDetailPriority
Remove or qualify delivery promise The "dag-til-dag" promise on the website is creating ~70% of all negative reviews. During busy periods, the "ekstraordinær travlhed" banner is not enough — orders should be blocked or the delivery selector should be hidden when capacity is constrained. Critical
Fix "ekspreshåndtering" upsell The express handling upsell (triggered by buying 6+ bottles) is described as a fraud in two detailed June reviews. Either deliver on the promise or remove the offer. The Jun 8 review documents that a B2B event order (same period) was fulfilled in 4 days while B2C express order took 12+ days. Critical
Proactive delay comms Multiple customers say they only found out about delays after contacting support themselves. Automated outbound SMS/email when an order hasn't shipped within 24h of the promised window would prevent Trustpilot reviews from the most frustrated customers. High
Pre-holiday capacity check Grundlovsdag (Jun 5) and Father's Day created the surge. Flag public holidays in the invite and order system — suppress express/next-day promises in the 5 days before major holidays. High
Campaign timing is working — maintain The invite-tp campaign maintained a 4.68 avg throughout the crisis. Invite timing (post-delivery confirmation) means invited customers had already received their wine before being asked. Do not change this mechanism. Maintain
Inventory synchronisation Two reviews cite products shown as in-stock that were not. The "smagskasse" customer had money debited on an out-of-stock item for 7 days. Inventory accuracy is a secondary but real issue. Medium
