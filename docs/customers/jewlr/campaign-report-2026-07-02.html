Strategy 1: Ticket-gated invites

Detection
Check HelpScout API: does this customer_key / email have an open or pending ticket?
Hold rule
Suppress invite send if ticket status = active or pending. Reschedule for +7 days after resolution.
Impact
Would have caught 34/161 (21%) of negative reviews. False positive rate: 16/357 (4.5%) of positive reviewers also had tickets — minimal suppression risk.

Strategy 2: Delay invite to 22–30 days

Current state
Invite sent at ~11–14 days (p50). Negative review rate: 18.8% in this window.
Proposed
Shift send to 22–30 days post-order. Negative rate in this window: 11.1% — a 42% reduction.
Benefit
Products with durability issues (stones, chain quality) surface within 7–14 days. Waiting 22–30d means problems are either resolved or the customer has accepted them.
Tradeoff
Slightly lower conversion rate expected as time-from-purchase excitement wanes. Recommend A/B test: 14d vs 25d send for 3 months.

Strategy 3: Product-level invite suppression / custom messaging

For JWLN0300 and other high-risk SKUs, the invite itself isn't the root problem — the product experience is. However, sending a proactive care email before the invite (at 7 days: "here's how to care for your necklace / size guidance") could shift sentiment before the review invite arrives. This is also an opportunity to pre-empt the return/exchange friction that drives 11% of negatives.
Action Items
  • 1
    Implement ticket-gated invite suppression. Before scheduling each invite, query HelpScout for open/active tickets on that customer email. Suppress and reschedule for 7 days after ticket closes. Based on 12-month data, this would have prevented 21% of negative reviews (34/161) with minimal false-positive suppression of happy customers (4.5% bleed). The signal is strongest for tickets arriving 2–14 days before the invite date.
  • 2
    A/B test 22–30 day invite delay vs current 11–14 day window. The 22–30d window shows 11.1% negative rate vs 18.8% at 11–14d — a 42% reduction in negative review rate. Run for 90 days across Campaign 101, splitting evenly. Monitor both negative rate AND total review volume to calculate net impact on TrustScore.
  • 3
    Escalate JWLN0300 (Personalized Name Necklace) product quality review. 38 negative Stamped reviews over 12 months, avg 2.0★ negative rating, 22.2% negative rate. Primary complaints: chain fragility, size smaller than shown, doesn't match photos. Recommend product listing update (add size reference photo, accurate chain gauge spec) and sourcing review of chain quality. Also cross-check with TP data — this SKU likely contributes to the "size wrong" complaint theme (largest TP complaint category at 13%).
  • 4
    Add proactive care touchpoint at day 7 for JWLN0300, JWLMB0013, JWL0515 buyers. Send a "caring for your jewlr piece" email at 7 days covering: chain care, size adjustment process, metal care to prevent tarnish. This converts a potential complaint trigger into a brand moment, and surfaces issues early enough to resolve before the day 25 invite.
  • 5
    Use support-intent ticket filter for prevention signal. The 323K ticket volume includes many automated email replies. Refine the ticket-gating rule to only suppress on tickets where the subject contains quality signals: "resiz", "wrong", "broke", "return", "refund", "defect", "problem", "damage", "tarnish", "missing". This reduces false suppression and sharpens the prevention signal.
  • 6
    Fix the customer service response gap. 17 negative TP reviews (11%) cite no response from CS. Several Stamped 1★ reviews also mention CS issues. The 149 currently active HelpScout tickets (from 30-day snapshot) should be triaged and resolved within 24h SLA, especially for quality complaints. CS resolution before invite send is the fastest path to prevention.
  • 7
    Extract order_id from ticket subjects systematically at ingestion. 81% of ticket subjects contain a parseable Jewlr order ID (format J + 10 digits). This should be extracted at HelpScout import time and stored in the order_id field. Currently only 3% of tickets have the field populated. This enables order-level ticket→review matching and makes the prevention signal much more precise (matching on order_id rather than just email_hash).
