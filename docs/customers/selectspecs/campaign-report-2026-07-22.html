Both windows are fully closed — the July window ended 5 days before generation.

Reading the rate honestly

The headline +105% rate uplift is real but flattered by two structural effects. First, the June denominator is inflated by the Jun 2–4 backlog blasts (~3,000 invites), many of which converted to reviews after Jun 17 — those reviews landed in later windows, including this July one. Second, July's 192 reviews include conversions from late-June invites (1,766 of July's 4,832 emailed orders received only reminders — their initial invite was sent in June). Both effects push the same direction seen in the Jul 2 report: the programme's true steady-state rate is likely in the 5–6% range, consistent with the 5.33% measured for Jun 18–Jul 1 which has since had time to close.

Escalation-worthy

Several 1★ reviews describe open, unresolved cases — undelivered orders with ignored refund requests and a month-old order still unfulfilled. These customers reviewed while their issue was live, the same pattern flagged on other accounts: invites reaching customers with open problems convert dissatisfied silence into public 1★ reviews. Two reviews from one customer used the word "scammers".

Key Findings

Efficiency, not volume, is driving reviews

July collected more reviews from far fewer invites: 192 reviews from 3,066 invited orders vs 166 from 5,451 — the invite-order review rate doubled (3.05% → 6.26%). Even on the most conservative denominator (all emailed orders including reminder-only carryover), the rate improved 2.95% → 3.97%. The programme's mid-June changes (reminder cycle, A/B templates) are holding up at steady state.

Invite volume nearly halved

Initial invites dropped 44% (5,453 → 3,066). Most of the gap is the June backlog blasts (~3,000 invites on Jun 2–4) not recurring — July's ~180/day average reflects real order flow. But daily sends are uneven (543 on Jul 1, then single digits on Jul 5–6 and Jul 12–13, 9 on Jul 17); worth confirming the send schedule isn't silently skipping days.

Rating quality slipped

Average rating fell 4.45★ → 4.33★ and 1★ share rose to 10.4% — the reverse of the improvement in the Jul 2 report (4.61★, 5.9% 1★ for Jun 18–Jul 1). The negatives are concentrated in fixable operations: wrong lenses fitted (9 reviews) and delivery failures (7) account for 59% of all negatives. These are order-verified customers, so the signal is genuine.

Recommended Actions

  • 1
    Escalate the lens quality-control signal to SelectSpecs. 9 of 27 negatives report wrong or unusable lenses — including one apparent prescription swap between two same-day orders from one household. This is the single largest driver of the rating decline and points at the glazing/dispatch QC step.
  • 2
    Suppress invites for orders with open delivery or support issues. At least 7 negatives came from customers whose orders never arrived or whose refund was pending. Gating invites on delivery confirmation / no open ticket would have prevented most of these public 1★ reviews.
  • 3
    Verify the send schedule. Daily invite volume swings from 543 to single digits with near-zero days on Jul 5–6, 12–13, and 17. If order flow is steady, sends are batching or skipping — smoothing this stabilises the weekly review inflow.
  • 4
    Adopt ~6% (invite-orders, closed window) as the steady-state benchmark. The Jul 1–17 rate of 6.26% and the now-closed Jun 18–Jul 1 rate of 5.33% bracket the programme's real performance at 5–6%. Track this metric monthly on closed windows rather than re-litigating the baseline.
