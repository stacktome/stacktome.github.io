Key Findings

Review volume

Invites drove a clear uplift in reviews: 186 vs 137 (a 36% increase in absolute volume) despite only 9% more invites being sent. Adjusted for invite volume, the review rate rose from 1.90% to 2.37% — a +25% relative improvement. As the attribution window matures and Jun 18+ reviews continue to come in through July, the rate gap is likely to widen further.

Rating quality

Invited customers leave significantly better reviews: average rating improved by +0.24 stars (4.37 → 4.61). The share of 5-star reviews grew +8.1pp (75% → 83%), 1-star share fell −2.9pp (8.8% → 5.9%), and the 3-star share collapsed −7.2pp (8.8% → 1.6%). This is consistent with the pattern seen across other accounts: order-verified invite recipients are primarily satisfied customers who simply needed a prompt to leave a review.

Attribution caveat

The baseline period (Jun 4–17) still contains reviews from invites sent before Jun 4 — meaning the true organic rate is lower than 1.90%, and the real uplift from invites is larger than the +25% measured here. A clean pre/post comparison will be possible in the next reporting cycle once the Jun 18+ attribution window fully closes (~2–3 weeks after last send).
