5. Observations & Next Steps

📌  Volume growth is healthy. Invite sends grew +40.8% (333 → 469) month-over-month. If May’s 9.91% review rate holds in June, that implies ~46 attributed reviews from June invites arriving over July–August — a meaningful increase in review volume.
📌  June email engagement is strong at 65% open rate. This suggests the positive-ticket audience is highly primed to engage. The 13.8% CTR is well above benchmark. If open and CTR are consistent with May (no API comparison available), review conversion should follow the same ~10% pattern.
⚠  3 low-rating reviews in May (9.1% of attributed reviews). For a "positive ticket" filter, any 1–2★ reviews are unexpected. These could indicate: (1) the ticket resolution was perceived as satisfactory by the agent but not the customer, (2) some orders had other unresolved issues, or (3) the review invite arrived alongside another negative experience. Recommend reviewing the 3 order_ids to understand the pattern.
📌  Campaign 712 (positive-feedback-tickets) is in pilot. 1 invite sent in June with no open yet. This appears to be a new variant of the programme. Monitor as volume builds — if it targets a different segment, compare its eventual review rate to campaign 442’s baseline of ~10%.
📌  Next evaluation point: mid-August 2026. By then, June invites should have had ~6 weeks to convert. Re-run this comparison with June attributed reviews to get a true MoM rate comparison. If June rate ≥ 8%, the programme is scaling well; if it drops significantly, investigate whether volume growth outpaced ticket-quality filtering.
