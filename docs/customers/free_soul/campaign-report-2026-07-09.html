Segment Setup Analysis

Current setup uses four segments targeting different customer cohorts. Below is an assessment of each against the key optimisation criteria.

recent-purchases (552)
→ invite-tp campaign 237
Window: 5–30 days post-dispatch
Ticket exclusion: Broad — excludes ALL customers with any ticket (even resolved 5★)
SKU exclusion: Partial — first-time buyers with FS-1-CREAGLOW, Veg-Choc-600, Veg-Van-600, Veg-SalCar-600, Veg-Ber-600 are excluded
Repeat buyers: Mixed — allows count_purchase = 1 for "good" SKUs; 2–5 for all SKUs. First-timers are included if they avoided the excluded SKUs.
Review quality risk: First-time buyers with taste complaints drive the majority of 1–2★ reviews in this campaign.
closed-tickets (554)
→ invite-closed campaign 240
Window: eventWindow=30 days, max 100/day
Ticket logic: Precise — all tickets closed, rating ≥5, no tp-skip tag, excludes rating ≤3
Repeat buyers: No purchase-count filter — ticket customers at any purchase count
Result: Perfect — 5.0★ average, zero negatives across 17 reviews. The strictest and best-performing segment.
Concern: Very low volume (327 invites / 4 months). Low eventWindow + maxEmails=100 limits throughput. If eligible pool is larger, volume is being throttled.
purchases-skus (652)
→ invite-skus campaign 310
Window: 5–30 days post-dispatch
Ticket exclusion: Broad — same as 552, excludes all ticket customers
SKU targeting: Narrow — only Veg-SalCar-600. Other excluded-from-552 SKUs (Choc, Van, Ber, Creaglow) have no dedicated campaign.
Repeat buyers: count_purchase ≤5 (includes first-timers)
Result: 4.44★ avg with 3 negatives, including 1★ taste complaint specifically about salted caramel. The SKU targeting concentrates the highest-risk first-time product buyers.
loyal-customers (845)
→ invite-loyal campaign 424
Window: 5–30 days post-dispatch
Ticket exclusion: Broad — excludes all ticket customers
Repeat buyers: Strong — count_purchase ≥6, not yet reviewed
Result: 4.61★ avg, 0.4% review rate. Rating is good but review rate is the lowest of all campaigns. Customers with ≥6 purchases either don't engage with email CTAs or are more considered in their reviews.
Concern: 43,525 invites but only 124 reviews. The "NOT reviewed" filter is correct, but the low CTR (0.5%) suggests email engagement for this cohort is weak.

Segment Optimisation Findings

Ticket exclusion — too broad in 552, 652, 845
Impact: all main campaigns
All three main segments use NOT (customer_key IN ticket) — this excludes customers who had a ticket at any point, even if it was resolved at 5★. Campaign 240 is supposed to pick them up, but with max 100 emails/day and eventWindow=30, many fall through the gap. Consider whether the ticket exclusion should be softened to "no open ticket" rather than "any ticket ever".
First-time buyers in segment 552 drive most negatives
34 of 51 negatives from invite-tp (campaign 237)
The SKU exclusion for first-timers covers 5 "difficult" SKUs, but first-timers with other SKUs are still invited. Taste complaints dominate the negatives — "salty", "too sweet", "powdery" — almost exclusively from customers who tried a product once and didn't like it. Setting min count_purchase ≥2 for segment 552 would remove this cohort. Estimated impact: significant reduction in 1★ reviews.
invite-skus covers only one of five excluded SKUs
Veg-SalCar-600 only
Segment 552 excludes first-time buyers with: FS-1-CREAGLOW, FS-Veg-Choc-600, FS-Veg-Van-600, Veg-SalCar-600, FS-Veg-Ber-600. Only Veg-SalCar-600 has a dedicated campaign (310). First-timers buying the other four SKUs receive no invite at all. If the intent is to capture all first-timers, the skus campaign should be expanded to include the other four SKUs — or alternatively accept that these are too risky to invite.
No branded-template A/B test on main campaigns
Campaigns 310 and 424 have branded templates at 0% weight
The `invite` and `reminder` branded templates exist in the workflows for campaigns 310 and 424 but are disabled. Campaign 237 (highest volume) has no branded variant at all. Campaign 240 uses branded templates but the 5.0★ baseline makes comparison with TP meaningless. To understand whether the Free Soul-branded email drives higher conversion than the TP template, a 20–30% weight test on campaigns 237 or 424 would provide meaningful data within 1–2 months.

Action Items

  • 1
    Test count_purchase ≥2 filter in segment 552 (recent-purchases) — First-time buyers are the primary source of 1★ taste complaints. Adding a minimum of 2 purchases to segment 552 would exclude single-order customers (who also have the excluded SKUs covered by campaign 310). Expected impact: meaningful reduction in 1★–2★ taste reviews from invite-tp without significantly reducing invite volume (repeat buyers dominate the eligible pool).
  • 2
    Expand invite-skus (310) to cover all 5 excluded SKUs — Currently only Veg-SalCar-600 first-time buyers are invited via campaign 310. First-time buyers of FS-1-CREAGLOW, FS-Veg-Choc-600, FS-Veg-Van-600, and FS-Veg-Ber-600 are excluded from both 552 and 310 — they receive no invite. Either expand segment 652 to include all five SKUs, or create a separate segment per product line if monitoring is needed individually.
  • 3
    Review ticket exclusion logic in segments 552, 652, 845 — All three main segments use NOT IN (any ticket) which excludes customers with resolved-positive tickets. These customers should ideally flow to campaign 240 for a 5.0★ review. However, campaign 240's maxEmails=100/day and eventWindow=30 is a bottleneck. Confirm whether the eligible pool for closed-tickets is larger than 327/quarter — if so, raise the cap to capture more.
  • 4
    Activate branded template A/B test on invite-tp (237) or invite-loyal (424) — The branded templates (invite, reminder) exist in campaigns 310 and 424 at 0% weight. There is no performance data comparing branded vs TP templates on the main campaigns. Set one campaign to 20% branded / 80% TP for Q3 2026 to establish a baseline. Campaign 424 (loyal, higher avg rating) is the lower-risk test bed.
  • 5
    Subscription complaints are systemic — flag to CX — 11 of 51 negatives relate to subscription experience: unexpected charges, inability to cancel (only pause), reminder emails missing or landing in junk. These are not invite-campaign issues but they are driving 1★–2★ reviews from otherwise loyal customers (invite-loyal cohort). The "stopped reminder emails" complaint from a 2-year customer is particularly notable. Escalate to the product/CX team for subscription UX review.
