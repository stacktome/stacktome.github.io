Segment Setup Analysis

Segment filters verified against live config. Each segment's actual SQL logic is summarised below.

recent-purchases (552)
→ invite-tp campaign 237 · 19,274 currently eligible
Window: 5–30 days post-dispatch
Purchase count: 2–5 purchases (primary path) OR exactly 1 purchase AND SKU not in excluded list
Ticket exclusion: NOT IN any ticket (any status, any rating)
SKU exclusion for 1st-timers: FS-1-CREAGLOW, FS-Veg-Choc-600, FS-Veg-Van-600, Veg-SalCar-600, FS-Veg-Ber-600
Upper cap: count_purchase ≤5 — buyers with 6+ flow to segment 845
Verdict: Well structured — 2+ repeat buyer filter already in place. First-timer OR branch is intentional: invites first-time buyers of non-risky SKUs.
closed-tickets (554)
→ invite-closed campaign 240 · 5 currently eligible
Ticket logic: All tickets closed AND at least one rated ≥5★ AND no ticket rated ≤3★ AND no tp-skip tag
Purchase count: No filter — any purchase count
Verdict: Precise and correct — 5.0★ average, zero negatives. Only 5 in pool today; pool resets daily as new tickets are closed.
Note: Current pool size (5) is very small — check if the eventWindow or maxEmails cap is throttling eligible customers who resolved tickets recently.
purchases-skus (652)
→ invite-skus campaign 310 · 933 currently eligible
Window: 5–30 days post-dispatch (draft pending: 15–30 days)
SKU targeting: Veg-SalCar-600 only
Purchase count: ≤5, no lower bound — first-time buyers of salted caramel are included from day 5
Ticket exclusion: NOT IN any ticket
Verdict: The only segment without a purchase-count floor. First-time Veg-SalCar-600 buyers are the highest-risk cohort — responsible for the worst taste reviews ("ABSOLUTELY DISGUSTING 🤮"). The pending draft (15-30 day window) adds delay but doesn't filter first-timers out.
Gap: The other 4 excluded SKUs (Choc-600, Van-600, Ber-600, CREAGLOW) have no campaign. Their first-time buyers get no invite — but Mar–Jun review data shows their repeat buyers rate well (4.13–4.77★) with complaints about subscription/delivery, not taste. Only salted caramel has taste-specific negatives.
loyal-customers (845)
→ invite-loyal campaign 424 · 17,495 currently eligible
Window: 5–30 days post-dispatch
Purchase count: ≥6 — strong repeat buyers only
Reviewed: NOT already reviewed
Ticket exclusion: NOT IN any ticket
Verdict: Clean and correct — 4.61★ avg. The 0.3% review rate vs 43k invites is a CTA engagement issue, not a segment logic issue. These customers are already deeply familiar with the brand and may not feel compelled by email review requests.

Segment Findings

Segment 652: no purchase-count floor for Veg-SalCar-600
Only segment without a lower count_purchase bound
Segment 552 protects against first-time salted caramel buyers via the excluded-SKU list. But segment 652 (the dedicated Veg-SalCar-600 campaign) has count_purchase ≤5 with no minimum — so first-time buyers land directly in this campaign. The pending draft only adjusts the dispatch window (15→30 days). Adding count_purchase ≥2 to segment 652 would match the logic already in place for every other segment, and remove the cohort most likely to write "I tried it once, it was disgusting."
4 excluded SKUs have no invite campaign
FS-1-CREAGLOW, FS-Veg-Choc-600, FS-Veg-Van-600, FS-Veg-Ber-600
First-time buyers of these four SKUs are blocked from segment 552 but have no equivalent of campaign 310. They receive no review invite regardless of how satisfied they are. If these SKUs are considered risky only for first-timers, expanding segment 652 (or creating a new segment) to include them with a count_purchase ≥2 filter would recover satisfied repeat buyers who are currently invisible.
Ticket exclusion is broad (any ticket = excluded)
Affects segments 552, 652, 845
All three main segments exclude customers who have ever had a ticket — regardless of resolution or rating. The closed-tickets campaign (240) is designed to pick these up, but with only 5 currently eligible and a small daily cap, high-rated resolved-ticket customers likely fall through. The exclusion logic itself is intentional; the question is whether the 240 campaign's throughput is sufficient to capture all of them.
No branded-template A/B test on main campaigns
Campaigns 310 and 424 have branded templates at 0% weight
Branded invite and reminder templates exist in the workflows for campaigns 310 and 424 but are disabled. Campaign 240 uses branded templates, but its 5.0★ floor makes the comparison meaningless. A 20–30% weight test on campaign 424 (loyal, lowest risk) over Q3 would yield enough volume to compare open and click rates between TP-hosted and branded emails.

Action Items

  • 1
    Add count_purchase ≥2 to the purchases-skus segment (652) — This is the only segment without a purchase-count floor. Veg-SalCar-600 first-time buyers are currently invited from day 5 and are the source of the most extreme 1★ taste reviews. The existing draft for the purchases-skus segment narrows the dispatch window to 15–30 days but still includes first-timers. Adding count_purchase ≥2 would align it with the logic already used in every other segment. If first-timers need a dedicated path, a separate segment with a longer wait window (e.g. 30–60 days) would be lower risk than inviting at day 5.
  • 2
    The 4 non-salted-caramel excluded SKUs look safe to open for repeat buyers — Reviewing Mar–Jun orders for the 5 excluded SKUs: FS-1-CREAGLOW (27 reviews, 4.37★), FS-Veg-Choc-600 (49, 4.41★), FS-Veg-Van-600 (44, 4.77★), FS-Veg-Ber-600 (8, 4.13★). Their negatives are overwhelmingly subscription and delivery complaints — not taste. Only Veg-SalCar-600 has exclusively taste-driven negatives ("disgusting", "powdery", "awful"). This means the exclusion of CREAGLOW, Choc, Van, and Ber from first-time buyers in segment 552 may be justified for first-timers, but repeat buyers of these SKUs (count_purchase ≥2) appear safe to invite. Expanding segment 652 to cover all 5 SKUs with a count_purchase ≥2 filter would capture these satisfied repeat buyers who currently get no invite.
  • 3
    Check whether the invite-closed campaign (240) daily cap is throttling the eligible pool — The closed-tickets segment (554) currently shows only 5 eligible customers. The campaign cap (maxEmails=100/day) and eventWindow=30 days means up to 3,000 invites per month could be sent if the pool were larger. With 327 invites over 4 months (avg ~82/month), the throughput is fine if the pool is genuinely that small — but if ticket resolution volume is higher, some eligible customers may be missed. Compare ticket closure volume in the CRM against the closed-tickets segment pool history.
  • 4
    Activate branded template A/B test on the invite-loyal campaign (424) — Branded invite and reminder templates exist in the invite-loyal workflow at 0% weight. The invite-loyal campaign is the lowest-risk test bed: 4.61★ avg rating, high-purchase-count audience (≥6 orders), and large enough volume (~11k invites/month) to see meaningful open/click differences within 6–8 weeks at a 20% split. This would be the first data point on whether Free Soul-branded email outperforms the TP-hosted template on CTR and review conversion.
  • 5
    Subscription complaints are systemic — flag to CX — 11 of 51 negatives relate to subscription experience: unexpected charges, inability to cancel (only pause), renewal reminder emails missing or landing in junk. These are not invite-campaign issues but they are driving 1★–2★ reviews from otherwise loyal customers (invite-loyal cohort). Notably, a 2-year customer gave 3★ specifically because renewal reminder emails stopped. Escalate to the product/CX team — this is a retention risk independent of TrustPilot.
