6. Complaint Themes (21 Low-Rated Order Reviews)

⚠ Theme 1: Customer Service Unresponsiveness (9 reviews)

The dominant complaint across all months: customers unable to reach CS by phone or email, being ghosted after raising issues, and having to chase repeatedly with no response. Multiple reviews mention 3–7 contact attempts with no reply. Affects returns, order status queries, and fault resolutions. Escalation language (“BBC Watchdog”, “consumer legislation”, “legal obligations”) suggests high customer frustration.

⚠ Theme 2: Delivery Delays vs Quoted Lead Times (7 reviews)

Orders consistently arriving weeks or months later than the quoted 4–6 week lead time. Examples: pendant ordered October, arrived April (6 months vs 8 weeks quoted); bathroom light promised in 3 weeks, arrived faulty 6+ weeks later; customer awaiting delivery for 4+ months with no proactive update. Post-Christmas period (Jan) was particularly affected. Delivery company Kinetic named specifically for cancelling deliveries 3 times and delivering to wrong addresses repeatedly.

⚠ Theme 3: Wrong / Incorrect Items Sent (3 reviews)

Orders arriving with wrong items: wrong lamp size, incorrect ceiling plate (wrong colour and design for a premium Nuura pendant), wrong order sent twice on repeated orders. Highlights a picking/packing accuracy issue and a need for quality checks on high-value orders.

⚠ Theme 4: Return & Refund Friction (3 reviews)

Customers struggling to get returns processed: initial refusal for damaged ex-display item citing “no returns” policy (overridden only after customer cited consumer legislation); £200+ pendant return ghosted for 4 weeks; store credit offered but rejected after repeated delivery failures. Refund process requires multiple escalations.

⚠ Theme 5: Misleading Information (2 reviews)

Moray wall lights: product photos showed amber hue that the delivered product lacked entirely (“looks terrible on the wall”). Bluelight card discount: website advertised 10% off with no minimum spend, CS corrected to £400 minimum without apology. Both cases involve customers making purchase decisions based on inaccurate product/promotional information.

10. Recommendations

Critical Improve CS response SLA. Unresponsive customer service is the single biggest driver of 1★ reviews. A committed 24–48h response time to emails and a staffed phone line during published hours would address the majority of complaint themes. Many customers are explicitly willing to give better reviews once issues are resolved — but they’re being left without any response. Would directly reduce 1★ reviews and improve monthly rating trend
Critical Review delivery partner (Kinetic). Multiple reviews name Kinetic specifically for cancelled deliveries and wrong-address drops. Repeated failures with the same carrier suggest a structural problem. Evaluate alternative delivery partners for large/premium items. Would reduce delivery complaint clusters in Apr–May
High Set realistic lead times and proactively communicate delays. Every month has reviews about items arriving weeks or months later than quoted. Customers don’t mind delays if they are kept informed proactively — they do mind having to chase repeatedly with no response. Trigger automated delay notifications when lead times extend. Converts delay-related 1★ reviews into 3–4★ through good comms
High Scale positive-closed-tickets campaign. This campaign generates reviews at 14.12% — 4× the rate of the main invite campaign. Currently only 170 invites sent in 5 months. Every positively resolved support ticket is an opportunity; automate the trigger more broadly. Most efficient review generator — direct volume increase
Medium Audit return & refund policy communication. Multiple customers were refused returns they were legally entitled to, only relenting after customers cited consumer legislation. Empower CS to accept legitimate return requests without escalation. Removes friction from a known complaint theme
Medium Improve product imagery accuracy. Moray wall lights received a 1★ review specifically citing that the projected light looked nothing like the photos. Review product images for accuracy, particularly for light colour/temperature rendering. Reduces post-purchase disappointment on visual products
