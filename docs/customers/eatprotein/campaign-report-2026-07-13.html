Methodology Note

Reviews are filtered to TrustPilot, order-level only (event_source = 'trustpilot', event_subtype = 'review_order') — this is the canonical review channel tied to the invite campaigns for both accounts. shake_that_weight also receives a much larger volume of order-level reviews via reviews.io (e.g. 946 in April alone vs. 51 on TrustPilot), spread fairly evenly across the month rather than a single bulk import — but these are excluded from this report so the two accounts are compared on the same review channel. Worth a follow-up if reviews.io should be tracked as a second channel going forward.

May figures show attributed reviews only — reviews matched to an invite send by joining on order_id against the account's all-time invite history (campaign 431 for shake_that_weight, campaign 432 for eatprotein). Unattributed May reviews are excluded from the May numbers throughout this report.

June figures show all reviews received, with no attribution join applied — per instruction, June is treated as organic-only this period, so every June review counts regardless of whether it can be matched to an invite. Note that invites were in fact still sent by both accounts in June (1,777 for shake_that_weight, 896 for eatprotein) — the "organic" framing here is a reporting choice for this comparison, not an observation that invite sends stopped.

Because the two months use different inclusion rules (May = attributed subset only, June = full population), the May → June comparison is directional, not apples-to-apples. Organic/unprompted reviewers tend to be either very satisfied or very frustrated — this alone can shift the average without any change in underlying product or service quality. For reference: shake_that_weight had 47 TrustPilot reviews in May (44 attributed, 3 unattributed) and eatprotein had 8 in May (3 attributed, 5 unattributed).
